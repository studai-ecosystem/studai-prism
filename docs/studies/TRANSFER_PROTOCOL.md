# Study 5 — Simulation-to-Reality Transfer

**Study key:** `sim_to_real_transfer` · **Status:** preregistered

## Hypothesis

Prism dimension scores correlate positively (r ≥ 0.4 target) with human-rated live group-exercise performance for the same candidates — the open question Google Research explicitly named for Vantage and did not answer.

## Design

- Partner college cohort completes BOTH: (a) a standard Prism assessment, (b) a live, in-person group exercise observed by ≥ 2 trained human raters using a rubric mapped to the same five dimensions.
- Live-exercise ratings are entered via a gated admin flow and linked to the Prism session (pseudonymous candidate_id linkage; ratings carry no PII).
- Live raters are blind to Prism scores; Prism scoring is unaware of live ratings.

## Sample

Target ≥ 50 candidates with both measurements.

## Preregistered metric

- Pearson AND Spearman correlation per dimension between Prism score and mean live-exercise rating.
- Disattenuated correlation (correcting for live-rating IRR) as secondary.

## Exclusions

Synthetic sessions; candidates whose live exercise had < 2 raters; integrity-flagged Prism sessions.

## Honesty constraint

Until this study reports, no Prism surface may claim real-world transfer — the certificate measures simulated-conversation performance, and public copy must say so.
